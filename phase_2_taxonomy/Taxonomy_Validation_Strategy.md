# Taxonomy Validation Strategy: The 4-Test Framework

To validate that the AROMA taxonomy "makes sense" (face validity) and is technically sound (structural validity), Isaac and Ethan should subject the 6-role model to these four tests.

---

## Test 1: The Discriminant Test
**Goal:** Ensure no two roles are redundant.
- **Protocol:** For any two roles (e.g., *Listener* vs. *Reflective Partner*), can you identify at least 3 conversational "marker moves" that belong to one but would be out-of-character for the other?
- **Failure Condition:** If roles are purely distinguishable by *Support Type* (D1) but not by *Relational Stance* (D2), they may need to be collapsed.
- **Reference:** [2.0_AROMA_Care_Role_Taxonomy.md](file:///Users/zac/Documents/Documents-it/AROMA/phase_2_taxonomy/2.0_AROMA_Care_Role_Taxonomy.md)

## Test 2: The Data Coverage Test (Exhaustiveness)
**Goal:** Ensure the taxonomy isn't missing a major role found in the data.
- **Protocol:** Select 10 random papers from the [Extraction Worksheet](file:///Users/zac/Documents/Documents-it/AROMA/phase_1_literature_synthesis/aroma_extraction_worksheet.csv) (filter for `D2 coverage`). Attempt to map the systems described to the 6 AROMA roles.
- **Failure Condition:** If >20% of papers require a "Misc/Other" role, the taxonomy is under-specified.
- **Check specifically:** Does the removal of the *Connector* role create a gap that *Navigator* cannot fill?

## Test 3: The Theoretical Alignment Test
**Goal:** Ensure the taxonomy honors its scientific foundations.
- **Protocol:** Cross-reference the [Theoretical Anchors](file:///Users/zac/Documents/Documents-it/AROMA/phase_0_theoretical_framework/0.1_Theoretical_Anchors.md) with the role definitions. Specifically, does the D2 definition follow the "Role-Taking" mechanism from Blumer (1969)? 
- **Failure Condition:** If roles are defined as static labels rather than turn-by-turn calibrated stances, the theoretical hardening from Phase 0 has failed.

## Test 4: The Paradox Stress-Test
**Goal:** Validate the **Authority-Agency Paradox** as a useful analytical lens.
- **Protocol:** Review the failure modes in [Zhang et al. (2024)](file:///Users/zac/Documents/Documents-it/AROMA/phase_1_literature_synthesis/aroma_synthesis_report.md#pseudo-intimacy-dependency-2-papers) and [Gabriel et al. (2024)](file:///Users/zac/Documents/Documents-it/AROMA/phase_1_literature_synthesis/aroma_synthesis_report.md#reliability-safety-gap-1-papers). Does the "High Paradox" label for the *Advisor* role accurately predict these failures?
- **Failure Condition:** If "Low Paradox" roles like *Listener* show similar agency failures, the paradox is not role-specific and might be a general system property.

---

## Next Steps after Validation
If the taxonomy passes these 4 tests, proceed to **Phase 3 (Qualitative Coding)** to establish Inter-Rater Reliability (Cohen's κ ≥ 0.70).
