# Phase 2 — Care Role Taxonomy

## Purpose

Defined the formal AROMA care role taxonomy using the empirical evidence gathered during Phase 1 (PRISMA Synthesis). This phase establishes the relational identities, activation conditions, and boundary conditions for each of the 6 core roles.

---

## Key Output

- **[2.0 AROMA Care Role Taxonomy](2.0_AROMA_Care_Role_Taxonomy.md)**: The definitive document defining the 6-role framework for conversational AI in mental health.

## Methodology

The taxonomy was developed using a hybrid conceptual-to-empirical method (Nickerson et al., 2013):

1. **Top-Down Stance Definition**: Relational stances were initially derived from Role Theory (Biddle, 1986).
2. **Bottom-Up Mapping**: 34 distinct literature terms mined from 203 papers were mapped to these stances.
3. **Inclusion Filter**: Roles were excluded or merged if they lacked ≥3 independent papers in the corpus (e.g., the *Connector* role was merged into *Navigator*).
4. **Theoretical Hardening**: Integrated Blumer's (1969) **Role-Taking** mechanism to define turn-by-turn enactment.

## The 6 AROMA Care Roles

| Role | Hallmark Stance | Theoretical Anchor | 
|---|---|---|
| **Listener** | Receptive / Non-directive | Rogers (1957) |
| **Reflective Partner** | Curious / Socratic | Feng (2009) |
| **Coach** | Directive / Forward-looking | Bandura (1997) |
| **Advisor** | Authoritative / Informational | Cutrona & Suhr (1992) |
| **Companion** | Warm / Co-present | Savic (2024) |
| **Navigator** | Practical / Resource-oriented | Gabriel et al. (2024) |

## The Authority-Agency Paradox

Phase 2 identifies a structural risk gradient across roles. **Advisor** and **Navigator** are identified as "High Paradox" roles where the AI claims a level of relational authority that it lacks the structural agency to exercise, creating a critical safety and ethics gap.
