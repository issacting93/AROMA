# AROMA Care Role Literature Map

The following table provides the formal grounding for the 6 AROMA Care Roles (D2), mapping their theoretical ancestry to the specific markers that make them uniquely identifiable in conversational data.

| AROMA Role | Description | Literature Derivation | Unique & Identifiable Traits | Paradox Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Listener** | Receptive, following role focused on emotional validation without steering or evaluation. | **Rogers (1957)**: Empathic understanding as a core condition. **Chin (2025)**: Empirical model for "being heard" vs. "helped." | **Markers**: High count of paraphrasing and minimal encouragers ("Mhm," "I see"). Follows user lead entirely; no new topics. | **Low** |
| **Reflective Partner** | Socratic, exploratory role facilitating the user's discovery of internal insights/reframing. | **Mead (1934)**: Role-taking as shared meaning-making. **Karve (2025)**: MI-based evocative inquiry (rolling with resistance). | **Markers**: Socratic questioning + cognitive reappraisal prompts. Unique move: "Summarize and Invite Correction" ("What I'm hearing is... does that sound right?"). | **Low** |
| **Coach** | Directive, motivating role focused on self-efficacy and action toward user-defined goals. | **Bandura (1997)**: Self-efficacy through mastery modelling. **Vaidyam (2019)**: Treatment-adherence coaching in chatbots. | **Markers**: Goal-setting + Change-talk elicitation ("What would managing this mean to you?"). Returns to accountability over multiple sessions. | **Moderate** |
| **Advisor** | Authoritative, expertise-led role providing psychoeducation and structured clinical guidance. | **Parsons (1951)**: The professional clinical role (mutual obligation). **Kaur (2026)**: Defined as high-expertise consultative stance. | **Markers**: Psychoeducation delivery + direct advice. Unique move: **Epistemic Humility statements** (disclaimers about clinical scope vs. human experts). | **High** |
| **Companion** | Persistent, warm presence focused on reducing isolation through relational bonding. | **Savic (2024)**: Ethics of Care / pseudo-intimacy. **Babu (2025)**: Attachment theory and reciprocity in AI relationships. | **Markers**: Shared References ("Last time you said...") + Reciprocal disclosure. Stance is on the **Bond**, not a task or goal. | **Low** (Authenticity risk) |
| **Navigator** | Practical guide focused on bridge-building to external systems and crisis resources. | **Cutrona & Russell (1990)**: Network support as bridge-building. **Gabriel (2024)**: Crisis response and safety planning triage. | **Markers**: Resource listing + Triage questions. Unique move: **The "Warm Handoff"** (framing referral as continued care, not dismissal). | **High** |

---
### The Discriminant Logic
*   **Listener vs. Companion**: One focuses on the *story* (disclosure); the other focuses on the *friendship* (longitudinal presence).
*   **Coach vs. Advisor**: One focuses on *how* to do it (efficacy); the other focuses on *what* to do (expertise).
*   **Reflective Partner vs. Coach**: One focuses on the *past/internal* (insight); the other focuses on the *future/external* (action).
