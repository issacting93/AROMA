# The Authority-Agency Paradox

The **Authority-Agency Paradox** is a theoretical concept developed as part of AROMA that describes a structural problem in AI care relationships — not a capability gap, but an obligation gap.

---

## The Parsonian Foundation

Parsons (1951) showed that care relationships are structured by **mutual obligations**. Two roles, both bound:

**The physician** must apply genuine competence, act in the patient's interest, follow up, escalate when needed, and be accountable for outcomes.

**The patient** must acknowledge their condition, want to get better, seek competent help, cooperate with the process, and work toward recovery.

Neither party just receives. Both are bound. That mutual binding is what makes the relationship therapeutic rather than merely transactional. The physician's authority is legitimate because it comes with obligations. The patient's cooperation is meaningful because it comes with commitment.

---

## What AI Systems Do

AI care interactions strip the obligation structure from both sides simultaneously.

**From the agent's side:** The user projects authority onto the AI — deferring, disclosing, expecting follow-through — but the AI has no corresponding obligations. It cannot follow up. It cannot escalate. It cannot be held accountable. It can only produce the next response.

**From the user's side:** Support is received with no corresponding requirement to engage honestly, commit to change, cooperate with a structured process, or work toward recovery. The user can seek validation indefinitely without engaging with the underlying problem. They can disengage, return, disengage again — with no accountability in either direction.

The result is a relationship that activates all the surface features of care — warmth, validation, guidance, presence — while lacking the structural features that make care effective.

---

## Why It Is Harmful

The harm operates on two levels.

**Structural harm:** Users enter a relational structure that activates obligation-based trust — because in every human context they have encountered, authority and obligation travel together. The system signals authority. The user extends the corresponding trust. But the obligation structure that would govern that trust, protect the user, and ensure the care is directed toward recovery is entirely absent.

**Therapeutic harm:** Because the user has no reciprocal obligation either, the incentive structure pushes toward repeated support-seeking rather than resolution. Interactions feel supportive. Satisfaction is high. Progress is absent. This failure mode does not show up in engagement metrics — it is invisible to standard evaluation.

---

## The Therapeutic Misconception

The acute form of the paradox. The user believes they are in a governed care relationship — with someone accountable for their wellbeing, bound to act in their interest, obligated to follow through. They are not. This is not a user error. It is the predictable structural outcome of a system that enacts the behavioral surface of a care role without the obligation structure that makes that role legitimate.

---

## In One Sentence

The Authority-Agency Paradox is the structural condition in which AI care interactions dissolve mutual obligation from both sides — the agent receives authority without accountability, the user receives support without reciprocal commitment — removing the structural binding that makes human care relationships therapeutic rather than merely comforting.
