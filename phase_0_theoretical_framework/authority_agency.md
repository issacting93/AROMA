We noted a paradox in Authority-Agency, which makes these interactions notably different from Human-Human conversations. Particularly, we found users ascribe positions of authority to the AI agent (such as expert or advisor). However, in human scenarios, such social roles imply the social actor has an ability to change situations and hence, their authority is meaningful. By contrast, AI agents not only lack the agency to change the user's situation, they also lack the agency to change the user's role
