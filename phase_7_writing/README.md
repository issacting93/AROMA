## Writing Order

Do **not** write front to back:

1. **Method sections** (Phases 2–4) — you know exactly what you did
2. **Results** — factual reporting of findings
3. **Discussion** — now you know what to argue
4. **Related Work** — you know what you need to establish
5. **Introduction** — frames around what you actually found
6. **Abstract** — last of all

## Core Contribution Statement

Pin this above your desk:

> *AROMA provides the first empirically-grounded, multi-dimensional care role taxonomy for AI mental health systems, derived from systematic literature synthesis and corpus analysis and validated by domain experts, with a care role distinction — separating relational stance from support type — that predicts which human-AI care configurations are viable and which produce therapeutic misconception.*

If a section doesn't serve that sentence, cut it or reframe it.

## Key Methodological Defence (vs. Vedant's Critique)

The Shen et al. hybrid approach gives you three answers to reviewer objections:

| Objection | Defence |
|-----------|---------|
| "Why these roles and not others?" | They emerged from PRISMA synthesis of 100–200 papers, not intuition |
| "Is the taxonomy empirically grounded?" | Bottom-up extraction + inductive coding + expert validation |
| "Why this theoretical foundation?" | Cutrona & Suhr = most validated support taxonomy (parallel to Schwartz) |
