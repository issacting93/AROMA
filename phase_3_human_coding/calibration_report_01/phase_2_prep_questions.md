# Coder Adjudication Guide: Phase 2 Preparation

These questions are designed to resolve the systematic "Divergence Signatures" identified in Phase 1. Review these before our next calibration session.

## Phase 1: Seeker Stance (Foundational)
*The primary confusion is between **Active** and **Passive** stances (κ=0.34).*

1.  **The Trigger Question:** At what point does a seeker's venting (Passive) turn into a request (Active)? 
    *   *Case for discussion:* If a seeker says "I don't know what to do," is that a Passive expression of despair or an Active invitation for advice?
2.  **The "Exploratory" Gap:** Why did we only find 2 "Exploratory" stances in 50 sequences? Are we missing moments of seeker self-reflection, or is it genuinely absent in ESConv?

## D1 & D2: The "Role Onset" Boundary
*63% of D2 disagreements involve the **None/Listener/Reflective Partner** triad.*

3.  **The "None" Threshold:** When does a supporter stop "just talking" and start "providing support"? 
    *   *Proposed Rule:* If they ask ≥ 2 targeted questions about the seeker's problem, should we mandate a minimum of "Listener"?
4.  **Acknowledgment vs. Synthesis:** Where is the line between a **Listener** (pure validation) and a **Reflective Partner** (paraphrasing/synthesizing)? 
5.  **The Directive Shift:** Coder A identifies significantly more **Advisors**. Coder A, are you coding "suggestions to try a coping skill" as advice, while Coder B sees them as part of a reflective dialogue?

## D3: Strategy Signatures
*Both coders under-detect "Restatement" relative to the ESConv ground truth.*

6.  **The Missing Restatements:** Why are we seeing 70% fewer restatements than the original ESConv labels? Are we only coding "perfect" paraphrasing and missing "partial" restatements?
7.  **Volume vs. Precision:** Coder A multi-labels strategies frequently (2-3 per sequence); Coder B sticks to single labels. Should we aim for "Main Strategy only" or "All observable behaviors"?

## Theoretical Alignment & Mismatch
8.  **Confidence vs. Agreement:** Both of you reported high confidence (Score 2) even in cases where you completely disagreed on the Role (e.g., Advisor vs. None). What was the "smoking gun" turn in those sequences that made you so certain of your choice?
9.  **The Directive Threshold:** Coder A, you frequently assign the **Advisor** role to seekers in a **Passive** stance (which the system flags as *Paradox Risk*). In your view, do these early suggestions serve a purpose even if the seeker hasn't explicitly asked for help yet?
